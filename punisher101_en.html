Punisher: Essential Readings

IT EN

1. Amazing Spider-Man (1973)

The Punisher debuted in 1973 within the pages of Amazing Spider-Man #129, created by Gerry Conway, John Romita, and Ross Andru. In a world populated by superheroes dressed in spandex and bright colors fighting for good, Frank Castle is an ex-marine and vigilante dressed in black with a skull on his chest, possessing a deeply personal concept of justice. After the massacre of his family in Central Park due to a gang shootout, Frank Castle decides to arm himself and fight crime as judge, jury, and executioner under the pseudonym "The Punisher." The Jackal hires him to eliminate his greatest adversary: Spider-Man.

2. Daredevil (1982)

Between the late '70s and early '80s, Frank Miller revolutionized the character of Daredevil, writing a cycle of stories with an urban and noir tone set in the smoky and disreputable Hell's Kitchen. After escaping from prison, Frank Castle tracks a group of drug dealers whose trade targets children and teenagers in Hell's Kitchen schools, clashing with Daredevil. This duel highlights the ideological difference between Matt Murdock and Frank Castle—between an unshakeable faith in law and rights, and someone who, having seen the distortions of a rotten and corrupt society, has decided to walk the path of personal justice. A fundamental run.

3. Punisher: Circle of Blood (1985)

The first solo trial: Frank Castle takes full control of the scene.

4. Punisher: War Journal (1988)

The war journal: daily chronicles of a vigilante.

5. Punisher (1990)

The aesthetics and storytelling of the 90s at their peak.

6. Punisher: Year One (1994)

The trauma: how the Central Park tragedy created the monster.

7. Punisher (2000)

"Welcome Back, Frank": Ennis brings dark humor and madness to New York.

8. Punisher: Born (2003)

Vietnam: where Frank's soul died and the Punisher was born.

9. Punisher Max (2004)

Influenced by the dark and paranoid atmosphere of post-9/11 America, Garth Ennis exponentially raises the level of violence with Punisher Max. Aimed at an adult audience, the series depicts a world without superheroes—gray and worn down by crime—a ruthless and hyper-realistic New York. Frank Castle is a sixty-year-old veteran with decades of war on crime behind him, aging in real-time page after page, a silent and unstoppable force of nature that reaps anyone in his path. A dark and nihilistic run in which heroes do not exist and evil is never fully defeated. The definitive series on the character.

10. Punisher: The Slavers (2005)

After saving a young woman hunted by the mob, Frank Castle soon realizes he has a very different case on his hands than what he is used to. Unlike classic gangsters seeking power, Frank stumbles upon a human trafficking ring from Eastern Europe led by Cristu Bulat and his father, a former Balkan war criminal who exploits and tortures women. Punisher dismantles the organization's machine piece by piece, gear by gear, exposing the degradation and brutality of the human being. Garth Ennis and Leandro Fernández deliver one of the most violent stories ever written about the character. A descent into hell from which no one returns.
